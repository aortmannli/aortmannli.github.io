Laghman

Laghman (Ù„ØºÙ…Ù†) is a Central Asian noodle soup dish traditionally cooked in a cast-iron pot called a kazan. There are many varietes of Laghman in Central Asia and this is my personal favorite recipe. It combines ingredients from both the Uyghur tradition as well the Uzbek. In Tashkent, my hometown, the Uyghur variety of laghman is the most popular. I hope you enjoy this recipe. ðŸ™‚

Soup Ingredients:

  • Spices:
    • 2 heaping tbs of Cumin Seeds
    • 2.5 Stars of Anise
    • 1.5 tsp of Coriander
    • 1 tsp of Paprika
    • Black and White Pepper to taste
    • Salt to taste
    • 2-3 Bay leaves
  • 600 g Lamb or Beef
  • 100 g Lamb Tail Fat
  • 350 ml Beef Broth
  • 1 Small Can of Tomato Paste
  • Veggies:
    • 7 Cloves of Garlic
    • 2 Potatoes - Medium Dice
    • 3 Tomatoes - Diced
    • 2 Sticks of Celery - Sliced
    • 1 Thick Carrot - Shredded
    • 1.5 Onion - Diced
    • 3 Orange/Yellow/Red Bell Peppers - Diced
    • 6 Radishes - Half-Slices
    • A bunch of Green Beans - Halved
    • 6 Small Chilli Peppers

Noodle Ingredients:

  • 500 g of oo flour
  • 200 ml water
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 tsp salt

Instructions

Noodles

  • 1. Combine water and egg and gradually work the mixture into the dough
  • 2. Knead until full combined
  • 3. Leave to rest for 30 minutes
  • 4. Cut into thick strips and rest for 10 min
  • 5. Hand stretch your noodles, rolling anbd pulling at the same time
  • 6. Butter the noodles and roll them as a spiral onto a large plate

Soup

  • 1. Cook meat and fat until brown. Remove extra liquid and store, will be added to the broth later.
  • 2. Add onion and half the garic and cook until golden. (will be used later don't throw)
  • 3. Add chili, potatoes, radishes, green beans, and half the spices. Mix around for 7-10 minutes.
  • 4. Add bell peppers, carrots, celery, and the rest of the spice mix. Mix around for 7-10 minutes.
  • 5. Add tomatoes and tomato sauce. Teaspoon of sesame sauce (optional). Allow to simmer for 5 minutes.
  • 6a. Add broth and 1/2 bay leaves. Wait 15-20 minutes.
  • 6b. Boil the lagman noodles. No need to salt/oil the pasta water.
  • 7. Plate and garnish with fresh cilantro and sesame seeds.

Guksi

Guk-si or Kuk-si (from Korean Guk-su êµ­ìˆ˜) is a Soviet-Korean cold noodle soup. It is essentially a vegetable broth over cold noodles, topped with a variety of salads. My family traditionally ate this dish twice a year, once on New Year's Even, which was the second most important holiday for us after Easter, as well as once at the start of the summer. Ingredients:

  • 1 Liter of Filtered Water
  • Spices (A lot of...):
    • Salt
    • Black Pepper
    • Gochugaru
    • Coriander
    • Dark Soy Sauce
    • White Vinegar
    • Sesame Oil
  • 500g Beef
  • 500g Cabbage
  • 8 Persian Cucumbers
  • 2 Heads of Garlic
  • 1 White Onion
  • 2 Bunches of Cilantro
  • 6 Tomatoes
  • 2 Red Bell Peppers
  • 2 Eggs
  • Sesame Seeds
  • Thin Noodles

Instructions:


Muri (Broth):
  • 1. Pour a Liter of cold, filtered water into a pot.
  • 2. Mince 2 medium tomatoes, 4 Persian cucumbers, and 1 bunch of cilantro.
  • 3. To the water, add 1 tbs of salt, 2 tbs of soy sauce, 4 tbs of sugar, and 2 tbs of white vinegar. Adjust to taste.
  • 4. Once you like the taste of the liquid mixture, add the finely mindex vegetables to the water and let cool. The longer you keep thead broth, the richer the falvor becomes.
Cabbage Salad:

*The cabbage takes the longest to make because you have to let it rest a lot. I reccomend you start prepping this salad first and make others while it rests.

  • 1. Cut the cabbage into very thin strips and salt and massage very generously. THis is supposed to draw out the water from the cabbage. Allow it to rest for 30 minutes to an hour until it is fully soft.
  • 2. Drain the cabbage liquid and rinse the cabbage in cold water. This step is very important. The extra salt shouldn't stay on the cabbage.
  • 3. Meanwhile, stir-fry minced garlic, onion, tomatoes, and red bell peppers until it looks like a sauce.
  • 4. Then add the cabbage and fry for another 10 minutes. Season with salt, black pepper, gochugaru, coriander, and garnish with minced cilantro.
  • 5. Let this dish cool in the refrigerator.
Cucmber Salad:
  • 1. Cut 4 Persian cucumers into very thin semicircles.
  • 2. Salt and leave to rest for 30 minutes until soft. (Do check on them every 10ish minutes because depending on the weather and stuff they might soften very quickly)
  • 3. Drain the liquid and rinse with cold water.
  • 4. Season with 1 tbs oil, 1 tbs soysauce, black pepper, gochugaru, coriander, minced garlic, and cilantro.
  • 5. Let this dish cool in the refrigerator.
Meat:
  • 1. Cut the beef into thin strips about 3cmx1cm
  • 2. Fill a pan (small kazan, preferably) with sunflower oil and let heat. If the oil sizzles when something is added to it (test with onion strip) then it is hot enough for cooking.
  • Fry the meat and diced onion until it is browned. Then, lwoer the flame. Add salt, black pepper, coriander, miced garlic, miched cilantro, anb a dash of gochugary.
  • Stir for about 1-2 minutes, then let cool.
Final Steps
  • 1. Boil the thin noodles. You can use somyeon, rice noodles, or anything else really. The only requirement is that they are thin.
  • 2. At the same time, make thin omelette using the two eggs and some salt and then cut it into thin strips.
  • 3. When the noodles are done, make sure to cool them immediately and wash them in some ice water. After that, drain and allow to dry.
Assembly
  • Pour the cold broth into a big bowl.
  • Add the cold noodles
  • On top, place the salads and meat to you liing. (Usually add about 3 heaping table spoons of each.)
  • Top with egg, sesame seeds, and semsame chili oil.

Shakshuka

Shakshuka (Ø´ÙƒØ´ÙˆÙƒØ©) is a North African and Middle-Eastern dish, often eaten for breakfast. It is often made in a cast-iron pan. This recipe is on the smaller side, for 2 people.

Ingredients:

  • 2 Eggs
  • 1/2 a Yellow Onion - Diced
  • 2 Garlic Cloves - Miced
  • 1 Red Bell Pepper - Diced
  • 1 Tomato - Diced
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin
  • 1 tsp Paprika
  • Pinch of Coriander
  • 1 Tbs Tomato Paste
  • Cilantro - to taste

Instructions:

  • 1. Finely Chop your vegetables.
  • 2. Fry the onion and garlic until golden.
  • 3. Add the spices to the oil and let infuse for 1 minute.
  • 4. Add the Bell peppers and stir until soft. Then add the tomatoes and the tomato paste to the mixture
  • 5. Make wells in the micture and crack the two eggs into them. Cover the pan with a lid until the egg is cooked.
  • 6. Garnish with olive oil, paprika, fresh cilantro, lemon juice, and watercress.
